    What is the pomodoro technique:

    A Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. The technique uses a timer to set intervals, normaly of 25 minutes, with breaks of 5 minutes. The name comes from the Italian word "pomodoro" which means tomato, in reference to the tomato-shaped kitchen timer Cirillo used. The idea behind the technique is to improve concentration and mental agility.

    How does it work?

      We recomend to follow the instructions bellow:
  • Write down the task you want to perfom in the todo list
  • Click on the task(s) you want to focus
  • Start the timer clicking in the "Tomato" button
  • After the 1st tomato rings, take a break(exercise, walk...)
  • After 3 tomatoes take a long break
  • Once you came back from the long break start over again
